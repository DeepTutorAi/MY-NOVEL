# 👥 Core Cast Biography

This document details the supporting cast, secondary POV figures, and key characters in the narrative, capturing their profiles, wants, needs, and final fates in both the main story and the far epilogue.

## ⛵ Ilyra Venn (Sky-Sailor / Active Present POV)

- **Age**: 23
- **Height**: 170 cm
- **Appearance**: Athletic and agile from rigging and repairing ships. Sun-baked skin with small scars. Brown-red/dark gold hair cut unevenly (cut it herself). Amber, defiant eyes. Wears a worn captain's coat, tall boots, and a utility belt with knives and tools.
- **Personality**: ENFP. Energetic, fast-talking, loves teasing Cael. Her humor is her shield: she talks to avoid silence and travels to avoid attachment.
- **Wound**: Worn by childhood memories of her family's sky-ship harvesting corpses from the Sky Ocean.
- **Want**: Prove that the Sky Ocean can be navigated, mapped, and understood.
- **Need**: Learn to respect nature rather than conquer it.
- **Route**:
  - *Early*: Joins Cael for curiosity/money, hiding her growing attachment.
  - *Middle*: Suffers ship damage and crew losses, realizing travel isn't the answer to everything.
  - *Late*: Burns her ship to light the path for the Elaris evacuation. She refuses to leave Cassian on the crumbling tower, but Cassian cuts the bridge rope, forcing her ship to drift away.
  - *Final*: Survives the finale and helps lead the survivors to build coastal homes on the new sea.
  - *Epilogue*: Dies of old age. Her journals, ship records, and the home she built by the coast with the presence of Cael's rain are preserved as historical landmarks.

## 🛠️ Maera Sol (Mechanist / Active Present POV)

- **Age**: 20
- **Height**: 163 cm
- **Appearance**: Small, thin, and agile build, but carries surprising strength. Hands marked by metal cuts, oil stains, and burns. Messy black hair cut short to chin-length (cut by herself to keep it out of her eyes). Light blue or silver-gray eyes that look at people as if evaluating where they are broken. Often wears a mechanic's shirt with rolled-up sleeves, a heavy leather tool belt, and can be seen carrying a leather glove in her teeth while working.
- **Personality**: ISTP. Direct, dry, and cynically practical. She is "unintentionally cute"—never sweet or affectionate, and refuses to state her feelings directly. Her warmth shows entirely through small, silent actions: fixing tools without being asked, remembering people's food preferences but pretending it was a coincidence, scolding people when she is worried about them, avoiding eye contact during rare moments of sincerity, and looking angry or shifting subjects when praised or embarrassed.
- **Core Contradiction**: Acts like she doesn't need anyone and prefers machines, yet silently tracks and remembers every tiny detail about everyone in the crew.
- **Want**: Keep the ship flying so they always have an escape route.
- **Need**: Accept that people are not machines; sometimes they do not want to be "repaired," they just need her to be present.
- **Route**:
  - *Early*: Irritated by Cael and Tovin, viewing them as loud and inefficient liabilities.
  - *Middle*: Forms a practical partnership with Tovin, letting him pass tools and assist with repairs. She begins to trust him silently, bandaging his minor injuries while calling him stupid.
  - *Late*: Stays behind in the engine room to manually severed the engine core of the *Albatross* to create the signal beacon, losing her left arm/hand in the process.
  - *Aftermath*: Survives the flood. Tovin cares for her, and they build a life together. Tovin tries to build her a mechanical prosthetic arm; it's crude and noisy, prompting her to scold his craftsmanship daily while refusing to let anyone else modify it.
  - *Epilogue*: Dies of old age. Her tools, city blueprints, and the crude prosthetic arm are displayed in a coastal museum, preserving her legacy as the engineer of the new coast.

## 🌾 Tovin Rusk (The Human Anchor)

- **Age**: 19
- **Height**: 174 cm
- **Appearance**: Solid, muscular build from heavy labor. Short, messy brown hair and warm, expressive brown eyes.
- **Personality**: ESFP/ESTP. The ordinary friend. Warm, talkative, funny, and easily frightened of dying, yet refuses to run away. He grounds the mythical characters in simple, human realities.
- **Want**: Keep Cael alive and remind everyone that Cael is a person, not a legend.
- **Need**: Accept that he cannot force Cael to choose safety over his destiny.
- **Fear**: That Cael will become a myth and people will forget he was once just a boy.
- **Route**:
  - *Early*: Follows Cael out of Elaris, acting as the team's emotional anchor. He immediately clashes with Maera's prickly nature.
  - *Middle*: Proves his value by assisting Maera, learning to anticipate her tool needs, and supporting her after the psychological horror of the Salt Ward.
  - *Late*: Separates from Maera during the evacuation to guide the children's group to safety, holding onto a signaling tool she gave him.
  - *Aftermath*: Reunited with Maera. He cares for her after she loses her arm, learning basic mechanics to build her a prototype arm. He becomes the primary witness and chronicler of "Cael the human."
  - *Epilogue*: Dies of old age. His records and chronicles form the basis of the true history of the rain listener.

## ⚔️ Cassian Veyr (The Veteran Leader / The Carrier / Mental Anchor / Deadpan Moodmaker)

- **Age**: 34
- **Height**: 188 cm
- **Appearance**: Broad-shouldered, tall, and exceptionally fast. Weathered skin with chemical/salt scars. Short black hair graying at the temples. Piercing steel-blue eyes.
- **Personality**: ENTJ. Disciplined, quiet, practical. He handles tactics, combat, and leadership without sentimentality. He is a strategic visionary who sees the long-term survival of the group as the ultimate goal, making decisions with cold logic.
- **The Mental Anchor**: A highly skilled leader whose aura shines through action rather than narrative exposition. In moments of panic (corroding hulls, beast attacks, split logistics), Cassian's heartbeat remains steady, ordering clear, executable tasks that anchor the crew's sanity. He provides a calm, protective shield that allows others to focus and survive.
- **Deadpan Moodmaker**: His situational comedy stems from extreme ENTJ literalism and military pragmatism. He is completely confused by civilian sarcasm, romantic bickering, and especially Tovin's clumsy, well-meaning "blockheadedness." His attempts to analyze illogical human behavior with cold tactical data create natural, unforced humor.
- **Want**: Save as many lives as possible, regardless of what he is called.
- **Need**: Realize that winning battles is useless if the systemic structure remains unchanged.
- **Route**:
  - *Early*: Saves the team from Reef-Hounds in a display of veteran skill, though the team initially distrusts him.
  - *Middle (Arc 3 - Team Cassian)*: Leads the infiltration of the Capital archives alongside Maera, Sera, Tovin, and Loran. He acts as the tactical pillar under pressure while constantly being baffled by Tovin's clumsy attempts to help Maera.
  - *Late*: Mentors Father Odran Vale, forcing him to channel his self-destructive guilt into active protection.
  - *Finale*: Makes a final stand on the falling tower. With Reef-Hounds and sea beasts closing in, he orders the last evacuation boat to leave. When Ilyra refuses to go, he uses his rifle to shoot and cut the bridge ropes himself, casting the boat off.
  - *Fate*: He turns back to face the beasts. His sword hand shakes slightly—not out of fear, but sheer exhaustion. He disappears in the collapse.
  - *Epilogue*: A legendary protector. His sword is found stuck on a small island that was once the summit of the tower. No one knows who placed it there.

## 🗺️ Sera Quill (The Cartographer / Active Present POV)

- **Age**: 27
- **Appearance**: Tall, thin, looking perpetually exhausted. Dark hair tied neatly, ink stains on her fingers.
- **Personality**: INTJ. Brilliant, systematic, sharp-tongued.
- **Want**: Document and preserve the absolute truth of history.
- **Need**: Stop hiding behind "neutrality." Erasing maps in a lying regime makes her complicit.
- **Route**:
  - *Early*: Works inside the archives, editing out flooded areas.
  - *Middle*: Discovers erased coordinates are mass graves; starts leaking files to Cael's team.
  - *Late*: Spreads the maps and structural truth to the public, forcing the migration.
  - *Final*: Survives the finale. She writes the uncensored, raw history of the flood, refusing to sanitise the events or make Cael or the others clean saints.
  - *Epilogue*: Her "True History of the Sky Ocean" survives as the primary text of the old world's demise.

## ⚖️ Father Odran Vale (The Penitent Priest)

- **Age**: 58
- **Appearance**: Tall, slightly hunched, with thinning grey-white hair and pale, sleepless eyes.
- **Personality**: INFJ. Quiet, mournful, wise. He does not ask for forgiveness, knowing it would be selfish.
- **The Catalyst**: Paralyzed by the guilt of signing child execution folders for the Ministry. His development is driven by Cassian, who forces him to turn passive, self-pitying despair into concrete action.
- **Want**: Atone for sending children to the Salt Ward.
- **Need**: Understand that seeking death is not true penance; he must live to carry the responsibility and act.
- **Route**:
  - *Early*: Acts as the team's guide through Ministry territory, hiding his connection to Nio.
  - *Middle (Arc 3 - Team Cael)*: Escapes with Cael and Ilyra through the Mistwood. He nearly collapses from psychological trauma until he is pushed to focus on protecting the mutated Nio.
  - *Late/Finale*: During the evacuation of Hallowmere, he locks himself in the gate mechanism room. He turns the rusty iron door wheel by hand until his finger bones crack. The sea water floods in, glass eels swimming past his ankles as children flee through the closing gap.
  - *Fate*: He drowns as the water fills the chamber, reciting the names of the children he once sent to their deaths. Before the water closes over his mouth, he speaks the final name in his booklet—not his own, but another victim's.
  - *Epilogue*: His booklet containing the list of victims is recovered and kept as a memorial of the cost of Hallowmere's survival.

## 🐋 Nio Arvane (The Brine-Touched / Past Flashbacks & Present Active POV)

- **Age**: 14 (at disappearance)
- **Appearance**: Extremely thin, skin translucent in parts, bones showing coral structures. pale blue eyes.
- **Personality**: INFP (before). Now gentle but thinks beyond human terms, speaking in whale frequencies.
- **Want**: Stop Cael from trying to force him back into a human form.
- **Need**: Have the autonomy to choose his own path.
- **Route**:
  - *Early (Past Flashbacks)*: Serves as the POV for the historical events 10 years ago, documenting the night he touched the forbidden rain, the onset of his mutation, and the horror of the Salt Ward containment cells.
  - *Middle (Present Active)*: Rescued in Arc 3. Serves as the active POV capturing his sensory shift into non-human frequencies and his silent communication with Veyra.
  - *Late*: Bridges with the Leviathan, forgetting human names and words one by one. During the finale, he completely forgets the word "brother," but sings the whale frequency of the melody Cael sang to him in childhood.
  - *Final/Epilogue*: Merges fully with the sea. He does not die like a human, surviving as a voice and a whale song heard in the bay on rainy nights.

## 🏹 Varek of the Red Sails (The Ruthless Hunter / The Dark Mirror)

- **Age**: 32
- **Height**: 185 cm
- **Appearance**: Broad, lean, with cold yellow-green eyes and short-cropped black hair. Face marked by a long scar running from jaw to collar. Wears heavy red leather gauntlets.
- **Personality**: ENTP. Brilliant, chaotic, and psychologically manipulative. Varek believes that order is maintained only through fear, unpredictability, and intellectual dominance. He is a dark mirror of Cassian: both are tactically brilliant, but while Cassian (ENTJ) leads through structured, predictable discipline and acts as a stabilizing anchor, Varek (ENTP) leads through psychological mind games, mocking authority, and keeping everyone off-balance. He treats the hunt as a high-stakes game.
- **Secret**: His hands under the red gauntlets are heavily mutated with black salt-scales, which he hides to avoid execution by the Ministry.
- **Want**: Hunt Cael's group to execute Ministry orders, solve the puzzle of the Tide-Key, and secure his own survival.
- **Need**: Realize that playing games cannot stop the descent of the ocean, and that defecting to Cael's group is the only intellectually interesting path to survival.
- **Route**:
  - *Early*: Relentlessly hunts Cael's crew, using mind games and terror tactics to scatter refugees.
  - *Middle*: His mutation is exposed at Pearl Colony. Knowing the Ministry will dissect him and finding their structure boringly predictable, he chooses defection, joining Cael's group out of survival and curiosity.
  - *Late*: During the flood, he defends refugees not out of compassion, but by brutally slaughtering Reef-Hounds and using terrifying mockery to force panicked civilians to remain orderly: *"Panic, and I will show you how Reef-Hounds skin their meat. Keep marching."*
  - *Final/Epilogue*: Mutates completely into a giant coral beast and vanishes. Remembered as "The Red-Eyed Terror" of the Mistwood—a monster-guardian that hunts sea predators and whom the coastal children leave red cloths to appease.

## 🧭 Loran Thorne (The Realist Navigator / Former Ministry Flight Commander)

- **Age**: 45
- **Height**: 176 cm
- **Appearance**: Weathered, sun-baked skin with a grey-streaked beard. Missing the middle finger of his left hand (lost during a Ministry dogfight). Smells of bitter tobacco and dried tea leaves. Wears a faded Ministry flight coat (with the insignia torn off), grease-stained trousers, and a heavy brass marine compass around his neck.
- **Personality**: ESTJ (Baan Fah / Sentinel). Structured, authoritative, realistic, and highly organized. Loran has no interest in grand heroic martyrdom; he views duty as a concrete calculation rather than an abstract ideal. He is blunt, hates grand speeches, but maintains high professional standards; he believes that protecting his family is the only real post-war duty. He possesses a keen analytical mind for logistics and risk, refusing to let emotion override survival calculations.
- **Want**: Escape the Ministry's collapse, take his daughter, and retire peacefully in the southern valleys.
- **Need**: Accept that defecting to protect his daughter is his true path of duty, and that he does not need to copy Cassian's path of martyrdom to pay his debts.
- **Route**:
  - *Early*: Hired by Ilyra in Arc 2 because of his deep knowledge of the Sky Ocean's Ministry patrol routes. His rigid military background is immediately spotted by Cassian.
  - *Middle*: Coordinates the flight paths of the *Albatross* alongside Cassian, using their shared training to outmaneuver Veyra's forces.
  - *Late*: At the end of Arc 4 (The Drowned Roads), upon analyzing the flight, wind shear, and structural data of the Sky-Dam Engine, he calculates a near-100% fatality rate for the mission. Recognizing it as a certain death sentence, he refuses to proceed. He directly confronts Cassian, hands Ilyra his flight charts, and departs at Bone Harbor to return south and evacuate his family. (This logical premonition is tragically validated when almost the entire remaining team is killed or permanently transformed).
  - *Aftermath*: Successfully moves his family to the southern mountains before the flood. He survives the disaster, settling as a respected elder in a new southern coastal village.
  - *Epilogue*: Dies of old age. He is remembered by his descendants as a smart man who chose life and family over the military lies of the Ministry.
