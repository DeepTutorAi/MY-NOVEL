# 🗺️ Setting Bible: The Sky Ocean & Reclaimed Earth

This document serves as the guide for the environment, cities, and wildlife of the world.

---

## 🌊 The Sky Ocean: Depth and Danger

The Sky Ocean is not uniform — it has five distinct depth layers with increasing pressure, darkness, and danger the deeper one descends. Most life and all human activity occurs in Layers 1-2. Layer 3 is the boundary of human exploration. Layers 4-5 are known only through brine-touched perception and the Dead Parliament's archives.

| Layer | Name | Depth | Danger | Inhabitants |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Sun-Sheet | 0–500m | Low | Silver Drifters, Pale-Skimmers, Cloud Whales (surface) |
| 2 | Drowned Quarter | 500m–2,000m | Medium | Lure-Lanterns, Husk-Takers, Reef-Hounds, Drowned Walkers |
| 3 | Pressure Veil | 2,000m–5,000m | HIGH | Trench-Wyrms, Spine Whales, Angler-Kelp |
| 4 | Old Pressure | 5,000m–9,000m | EXTREME | Abyssal Leviathans, Void-Anglers |
| 5 | First Memory | 9,000m+ | BEYOND | The First Leviathan only |

The *Albatross* never descends below Layer 2 under normal operation. The emergency descent to approach the Sky-Dam's underwater foundations (Arc 5) briefly touches Layer 3 — the first time any of the crew experiences true deep-ocean pressure.

**Key Locations within the Layers:**
- **The Sunken Quarter** (Layer 2): An intact district of the Ascendant Engineers' original city — streets are vertical shafts. The whale-bone inscription was found here.
- **The Explorer's Graveyard** (Layer 2/3 boundary): Dozens of wrecked exploration vessels drifting in a permanent current. Ministry ships among them — proof of their secret exploration program.
- **The Glimmer Shelf** (Layer 3): A vast living bioluminescent coral reef pulsing in rhythm with the Leviathan's heartbeat. Sera theorizes it is an extension of the Leviathan's nervous system.

## 🦠 Parasites & Mutated Wildlife

### The Core Parasite: Brine-Spores
Rain from the Sky Ocean carries **Brine-Spores** — microscopic parasitic organisms. On skin contact, they burrow into the bloodstream within minutes and begin migrating toward the central nervous system. The parasite does not kill. It transforms the host into a hybrid organism capable of surviving in the sea — gills, coral-reinforced bones, webbing, whale-frequency speech. The host's consciousness is preserved. **Nio is still Nio** — he just experiences reality through biology that is no longer exclusively human. The Ministry calls the infected "brine-touched." The Chorus calls it "being chosen by the sea."

### Other Parasites
- **Coral-Spores**: Parasitic coral that replaces the host's skeleton with living coral. The host remains conscious. Cultivated by the Warden of Pearl.
- **The Warden's Bloom**: An engineered parasite that converts ALL tissue into a unified calcium-carbonate structure — living statues, fully conscious, immobile for decades.
- **Bone-Silt Borer**: Parasitic worm targeting bone marrow. Replaces marrow tissue with brittle calcium deposits.
- **Glass Eels**: Swim in pipes and human bloodstreams. Function as transmitters for the Leviathan's voice. Brine-Spore-infected hosts attract them.

### Mutated Wildlife

- **The First Leviathan**: The primal mind of the ancient ocean. It communicates not with words, but with sensory images. To look at it directly causes an overwhelming awareness of the ecological system that a human mind cannot process.
- **The Choir-Warden**: The physical form of the Drowned Choir — a guardian entity at the Sky-Dam threshold. Hundreds of preserved human figures in a wall of brine-saturated water. Created from the accumulated consciousnesses of Brine-Spore hosts harvested by the Dead Parliament. Tests intruders with one question: *"Are you the sea, or are you the thief?"*
- **Cloud Whales**: Massive cetaceans that have adapted to swim through the Sky Ocean and cloud layers. Their bones bear ancient scars and metal fittings from when the Ascendant Engineers anchored them to support the Sky-Dam.
- **Pale-Moths**: Tiny translucent moths that appear on battlefields and deathbeds. They feed on the "final breath" of dying creatures and are viewed as omens the sea is claiming a soul.
- **Reef-Hounds**: Quadrupedal predators with coral growths forming their skulls. Mutated from domestic dogs left behind in drowned ruins, they still possess faint instincts of their past lives (e.g. guarding ruined doorways).
- **Glass Eels**: Translucent, thread-like fish that swim in pipe systems and the bloodstreams of those who are brine-touched. They act as transmitters for the sea's voice.

## 🏛️ Major Cities & Infrastructure

- **The Sky-Dam Engine**: A colossal structure of bone, shell, and copper built by the Ascendant Engineers. It holds the ocean aloft. Inside, pipes beat like blood vessels, and the consciousness of the Dead Parliament speaks through metal grates.
- **Elaris**: Cael's hometown. A mountain city built on steep cliffs, sitting directly above the geological fault line where the sea was torn from the earth.
- **The Salt Ward**: A heavily guarded detention laboratory beneath the Sky-Dam where the Ministry holds Brine-Spore-infected children — studying the parasite's life cycle, attempting to weaponize it.
- **Hallowmere**: A religious city governed by the Ministry, where citizens believe the rising flood is a holy baptism and refuse to evacuate.
- **Nacre-Vault**: A wealthy city that harvests "pearls" from the bones of brine-touched corpses, profiting off the mutation and actively resisting the dam's destruction.

## 🌲 Reclaimed Wilderness (The Drowned Roads)

- **The Mistwood**: A woodland where trees draw moisture from the fog. Their leaves are fleshy and rib-like, breathing in unison during the humid nights.
- **The Pearl Colony**: A settlement where Brine-Spore hosts infected with Coral-Spores or the Warden's Bloom undergo calcification — their bodies slowly transformed into living coral and calcium-carbonate structures.
- **The Upside Reef**: A massive coral reef hanging down from the sky-cliffs, preying on birds, sky-ships, and the memories of travelers.
- **The Bone Harbor**: An ancient shipyard buried in dry mud. When the rain falls, the dried hulls begin to float out of the dirt, remembering their past.
