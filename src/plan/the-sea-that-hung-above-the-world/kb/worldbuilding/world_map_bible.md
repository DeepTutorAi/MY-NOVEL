## PART 1: MAIN ROUTE LOCATIONS (ทีมหลักไปจริง)

---

### 1. Elaris
- **Type**: Mountain city — Cael's hometown
- **Geography**: Built on steep mountain cliffs. Sits directly on the geological fault line where the ocean was torn from the earth 1,000 years ago. The ground beneath the city is the old seabed. When the Sky Ocean falls, Elaris is the first place the water will hit.
- **Relative Position**: Southeast of the Sky-Dam. ~200 km by airship = 1-2 days flight. By foot from Capital: 4-5 days through mountain passes.
- **Who Controls**: Ministry — via a local governor. Inquisitors patrol regularly. The population is kept compliant through fear of rain and brine-touch.
- **Local Features**: Drainage systems built into the mountain — Cael works as a Rain Listener cleaning these grates. The pipes amplify sounds from deep within the mountain. Elder Soll's house at the edge of town — the only place with books older than the Ministry's censorship.
- **Importance to Plot**: Where the story begins. Cael hears Nio's voice here. The first village destroyed by rain is nearby. The crew departs from Elaris docks.
- **First Appearance**: Arc 1 Ch 1
- **Last Appearance**: Arc 6 Ch 26 — evacuated. Flooded by the Descent Zone.
- **Key Events**:
  - Cael hears Nio in the pipes (Ch 1)
  - Flashback: Nio touches forbidden rain (Ch 2)
  - Ilyra's *Albatross* docked for repairs (Ch 3)
  - Elder Soll reveals Rain Listener lineage (Ch 5)
  - Departure — Isolde's silent farewell (Ch 5)
  - Evacuation — Garrick & Lisse's baby born on the escape ship (Ch 26)
- **Final State**: Flooded. The mountain peak becomes an island. The drainage pipes where Cael first heard Nio are now underwater. Nio's whale song sometimes echoes from them on rainy nights.

---

### 2. The Sky Ocean (ทะเลฟ้า)
- **Type**: Ecosystem — Suspended ocean
- **Geography**: The entire ancient ocean, lifted to ~8,000-12,000 meters altitude. Not just water — a complete marine ecosystem: fish, whales, coral, currents, weather systems. The surface is navigable by airship. The depths are unknown — no one has gone deep and returned.
- **Relative Position**: Above everything. Separated from the earth by the Shroud (mist barrier). Covers approximately the same area as the original ocean — most of the visible sky.
- **Who Controls**: No one. The Dead Parliament controls the Sky-Dam that holds it up. The Sky Ocean itself is uncontrollable.
- **Importance to Plot**: The setting of all travel between locations. Contains drowned ruins (upside-down cities from before the lift). The Leviathan resides in its deepest parts.
- **First Appearance**: Arc 2 Ch 6 — Cael surfaces through the Shroud for the first time.
- **Last Appearance**: Arc 7 Ch 33 — Falling. The ocean descends to earth.
- **Key Events**:
  - Cael's first sight: *"It's like… the sky turned upside down."* (Ch 6)
  - Discovery of drowned ruins and whale-bone inscription (Ch 7)
  - Varek's pursuit across the surface (Ch 8-9)
  - Return journey toward Sky-Dam (Ch 20)
- **Final State**: Returned to earth. The new ocean covers the old seabed. The Sky-Dam's scar remains in the atmosphere — a permanent reminder in the sky.

## THE SKY OCEAN DEPTH LAYERS (ชั้นความลึกของทะเลฟ้า)

The Sky Ocean is not a uniform body of water. It has five distinct depth layers, each with its own ecosystem, pressure conditions, and danger level. The deeper you descend, the closer you come to the Leviathan's core frequency — and the more the ocean remembers what it was before it was lifted.

The layers are known by what sailors and explorers who survived each one reported. Most airships never descend below Layer 2. No one has reached Layer 5 and returned as themselves.

---

### LAYER 1: The Sun-Sheet (แผ่นตะวัน) — 0 to 500m depth
- **Altitute Range**: ~12,000m → 11,500m (from the Sky Ocean surface)
- **Description**: The sunlit surface of the Sky Ocean. Warm. Bright. Navigable by any airship with a sealed hull. This is where the *Albatross* operates for most of the story. Fish are small — silver-scaled and fast. Cloud Whales surface here to breathe. Ash-Blooms form on calm days.
- **Visibility**: Clear — 30-50 meters on good days.
- **Fauna**: Normal-seeming fish (Silver Drifters, Pale-Skimmers), Cloud Whales (surface), Pale-Moths (surface swarms), Shade-Flame (at dusk).
- **Danger Level**: Low. The surface is the safest part of the Sky Ocean. Storms and Ash-Blooms are the primary hazards.
- **Human Presence**: Frequent — airship trade routes cross Layer 1. The Ministry patrols here.
- **Arc Appearances**: Arc 2 (entire arc), Arc 3 (Team Cael's flight path), Arc 4 (travel routes)
- **Narrative Use**: The "normal" baseline. The beauty of the Sky Ocean. The reader's comfort zone before the descent.

---

### LAYER 2: The Drowned Quarter (ย่านจมน้ำ) — 500m to 2,000m depth
- **Description**: The ruins of cities that were lifted with the ocean 1,000 years ago. Buildings hang upside-down in the water — their foundations anchored to the ocean floor, their rooftops pointing toward the earth below. Streets are vertical shafts. Windows are portals into dark rooms where things still move.
- **Visibility**: Dim — 10-20 meters. Bioluminescent moss on the ruins provides faint blue-green light.
- **Fauna**: Reef-Hounds (nest in abandoned buildings), Lure-Lanterns (first appearance), Husk-Takers (nest in doorways), Ghost-Coral formations, Glass Eels (migrating).
- **Danger Level**: Medium. Creatures here are predators, not scavengers. The ruins provide ambush terrain. The Lure-Lanterns use light to draw prey into buildings.
- **Human Presence**: Rare — only salvage crews (like Ilyra's family) and desperate explorers. The Ministry claims to patrol here but rarely does. The Corpse Fields drift at this depth.
- **Arc Appearances**: Arc 2 Ch 7 (the whale-bone inscription is in a Layer 2 ruin), Arc 5 (Ilyra's detour to the Sunken Archive)
- **Key Locations within Layer 2**:
  - **The Sunken Quarter**: An entire district of the Engineers' old city. Streets are vertical — explorers rappel between balconies. The whale bone with the inscription "The ocean was not lifted. It was butchered upward" is embedded in the central plaza.
  - **The Corpse Fields**: A drifting mass of preserved bodies — some ancient, some recent. The Drowned Choir originates here. Pale-Moths breed here.
  - **The Sunken Archive**: Ascendant Engineer library. Upper spire visible from Layer 1. Ilyra's family salvaged from here.
- **Narrative Use**: Adventure + horror blend. Discovery zone. The mystery of "what happened here" deepens.

---

### LAYER 3: The Pressure Veil (ม่านแรงดัน) — 2,000m to 5,000m depth
- **Description**: The transition zone. Light from above has faded to a permanent twilight. Pressure increases — standard airship hulls cannot descend below 2,500m without reinforcement. The water is denser here — sounds travel differently. Voices echo with a metallic ring. The Tide-Singing phenomenon originates from this layer.
- **Visibility**: 5-10 meters. Bioluminescent organisms provide patchy illumination. The water itself glows faintly blue — a phenomenon called "Deep-Glow."
- **Fauna**: Angler-Kelp (predatory plant — lures with light), Spine Leviathans (juvenile — 5-15m), Trench-Wyrms (serpentine pack hunters), Glass Eels (dense schools — their combined bioluminescence creates false constellations), Deep-Glow plankton (the source of the ambient light).
- **Danger Level**: HIGH. Creatures here are adapted to darkness and pressure. Angler-Kelp mimics the lights of a settlement to lure ships. Trench-Wyrms hunt in coordinated packs with tactical intelligence comparable to wolves. Spine Leviathans — juveniles of the species that may be related to the First Leviathan — are curious and will approach ships, sometimes damaging hulls accidentally.
- **Human Presence**: None. No recorded successful expedition. The Ministry lists Layer 3 as a "prohibited zone" — officially because of danger, unofficially because they do not want anyone finding what is down there.
- **Arc Appearances**: Arc 5 Ch 21-22 — the *Albatross* must briefly descend into Layer 3 to approach the Sky-Dam's underwater foundations. This is the first time any of the crew has been this deep. The descent requires Maera to reinforce the hull with emergency seals. Loran's charts mark Layer 3 boundaries with warning symbols.
- **Narrative Use**: The "point of no return." First true taste of the Sky Ocean's abyssal danger. The crew realizes the ocean is not empty — it is watching.

---

### LAYER 4: The Old Pressure (แรงดันโบราณ) — 5,000m to 9,000m depth
- **Description**: Complete darkness except for bioluminescent organisms. Pressure is crushing — only specialized submersibles (which do not exist in this world) or brine-touched physiology can survive here. The water is cold — near freezing. The temperature difference creates permanent currents that spiral downward — "The Descent Spiral." Anything that falls into Layer 4 never rises.
- **Visibility**: 0-3 meters. Only bioluminescent flashes — brief, unpredictable, and usually predatory.
- **Fauna**: Abyssal Leviathans (fully grown — 30-80m), Void-Anglers (gigantic version of Layer 3 Angler-Kelp — lures mimic whole ships), The Fossil Drift (not living — the calcified remains of ancient sea creatures from before the lift, suspended in the pressure current), Bone-Sifters (scavengers that feed on the Fossil Drift).
- **Danger Level**: EXTREME. Survival is not expected. The Abyssal Leviathans are the largest known predators in the Sky Ocean — they may be related to the First Leviathan, or they may be its "children." Their songs are what brine-touched hear as the Leviathan's call.
- **Human Presence**: Zero. No human has reached Layer 4 and returned.
- **Arc Appearances**: Arc 5 Ch 25 — Nio, in his brine-touched state, perceives Layer 4 through the Leviathan's senses during his connection to the Dead Parliament. He sees the Abyssal Leviathans circling in the deep — and realizes they are not hostile. They are waiting.
- **Narrative Use**: Revelation, not combat. The deep ocean is not an enemy — it is a living system. The Abyssal Leviathans are guardians of the old memory. Nio's perception of this layer is the first hint that the Leviathan is not evil — it is the custodian of something ancient.

---

### LAYER 5: The First Memory (ความทรงจำแรก) — 9,000m to the seafloor (~12,000m+)
- **Description**: The absolute bottom of the Sky Ocean. The water here is not water — or rather, it is water that has been saturated with the Leviathan's consciousness for 1,000 years. The boundary between physical reality and the Leviathan's perception dissolves. Time does not flow normally. The "seafloor" is the original seabed from before the lift — a landscape of fossilized coral mountains, ancient trenches, and the foundation anchors of the Sky-Dam Engine.
- **Visibility**: Unmeasurable — perception here is not visual. The Leviathan's consciousness provides a kind of "sight" that is actually total sensory immersion — you feel the temperature of every current, the hunger of every predator, the memory of every death.
- **Fauna**: The First Leviathan (the only confirmed entity). Possible others — the consciousness archive of extinct species. No physical predators survive at this depth — the Leviathan's presence is incompatible with normal biology.
- **Danger Level**: BEYOND MEASUREMENT. Not because of physical threat — because of perceptual overload. The Leviathan does not attack. It perceives. And sharing that perception with a human mind is like pouring the ocean into a cup.
- **Human Presence**: One — Cael Arvane, through the Tide-Key Engine connection. His consciousness touches Layer 5 as he becomes the Tide-Key. He does not "see" the Leviathan — he "understands" it for one eternal second before his human self dissolves.
- **Arc Appearances**: Arc 5 Ch 25 (Nio glimpses it), Arc 7 Ch 32 (Cael touches it), Arc 7 Ch 34 (Nio merges with it).
- **Narrative Use**: The climax of the mystery. The Leviathan is not a monster — it is memory itself. Cael's sacrifice is not killing it or defeating it — it is becoming part of the system that releases it back to the earth.

### 🌊 SKY OCEAN: ADDITIONAL LOCATIONS WITHIN LAYERS

### 2-A. The Sunken Quarter (ย่านจมน้ำ) — Layer 2
- **Type**: Drowned urban district — Ascendant Engineer civilization remnants
- **Geography**: A preserved section of the original city that existed before the ocean was lifted. Buildings are intact but inverted — their roofs point downward toward earth. Streets are vertical shafts where explorers rappel between balconies. Windows reveal furnished rooms — tables still set, books still on shelves, preserved by salt and pressure. Some rooms have occupants.
- **Danger**: Husk-Takers nest in doorways — they imitate human silhouettes. Lure-Lanterns pulse from deep windows like candlelight. Reef-Hounds den in the lower buildings.
- **Explorer Evidence**: Salvage crews have marked "safe routes" with painted symbols — but some symbols are fake, drawn by Mimics. Ilyra's family crest is painted on one building — her father's expedition reached here.
- **First Appearance**: Arc 2 Ch 7 — The *Albatross* drifts over the Sunken Quarter. Cael sees lights moving in the windows. Cassian: *"Those aren't lights. Don't look at them."*
- **Narrative Use**: The first proof that a civilization existed here. The whale-bone inscription is found in the central plaza.

---

### 2-B. The Explorer's Graveyard (สุสานนักสำรวจ) — Layer 2/3 boundary
- **Type**: Shipwreck accumulation zone
- **Geography**: A permanent current at the 5,000m boundary captures ships that attempted to return from Layer 3 but ran out of power. Dozens of vessels — some centuries old, some recent — drift in a slow spiral. Their hulls are intact. Their crews are still inside — preserved by salt and pressure. Some crews appear to have died fighting each other.
- **Danger**: Hull-Barnacles cover every wreck. Keening Crabs nest in the hulls by the thousands — the graveyard is permanently filled with the sound of crying. Some wrecks still have functional lights — Lure-Lanterns have colonized them and mimic distress signals.
- **Evidence**: Among the wrecks are Ministry scientific vessels — proof that the Ministry has been exploring the depths for decades. One ship bears the insignia of the Harvesters division.
- **First Appearance**: Arc 5 Ch 21 — The *Albatross* drifts past the outer edge of the Graveyard during the emergency descent to avoid detection. Ilyra recognizes one of the wrecks — it belonged to a captain her father knew.
- **Last Appearance**: Arc 7 Ch 33 — When the ocean falls, the Graveyard falls with it. The wrecks finally reach the earth they were trying to return to.
- **Narrative Use**: Atmospheric horror. Visual proof of how many have tried and failed. The Ministry's secret exploration program revealed.

---

### 2-C. The Glimmer Shelf (หิ้งแสง) — Layer 3
- **Type**: Bioluminescent reef — living structure
- **Geography**: A vast shelf of living bioluminescent coral at 3,000m. The coral emits soft blue-green light in rhythmic pulses — synchronized across the entire shelf. The pulses match the Leviathan's heartbeat frequency. From above, it looks like a city of light on the ocean floor — but it is not a city. It is alive.
- **Danger**: The coral is carnivorous — it feeds on small fish and anything that touches its surface. The light pulses are hypnotic — creatures (and humans) who stare too long become disoriented and drift into the coral.
- **Origin/Lore**: Sera's theory: the Glimmer Shelf is a "nervous system extension" of the Leviathan — a sensory organ grown across the seafloor. The Ministry's classified documents call it "Substrate Zero" and have been trying to reach it for decades.
- **First Appearance**: Arc 5 Ch 22 — Nio perceives the Glimmer Shelf during his initial contact with the Dead Parliament. He describes it as *"a city of light that breathes."*
- **Last Appearance**: Arc 7 Ch 34 — Falls with the ocean. The coral survives — it becomes the foundation of the new sea's reef system.
- **Narrative Use**: Thematic resonance — the ocean is one connected organism. The Glimmer Shelf is proof that the Leviathan is not a single creature but the consciousness of an entire ecosystem.

### 3. The Capital / Ministry Headquarters
- **Type**: Government center
- **Geography**: Built on a plateau east of the Sky-Dam. The city is constructed on what was once the continental shelf — the ground is compacted seabed. Architecture is stone and iron — heavy, oppressive, designed to resist salt corrosion.
- **Relative Position**: East of the Sky-Dam. ~1.5 days by airship from Elaris. Connected to the Salt Ward via underground tunnels.
- **Who Controls**: Ministry of Dry Earth — Marrow Saint's seat of power. Inquisitor headquarters. Military command center (Commander Voss).
- **Local Features**: The Grand Library (Sera's workplace), the Archive Wing (where Sera and Lienn work), the Steam Pipeline network (Team Cassian's escape route), the broadcast tower (where Sera transmits the truth).
- **Importance to Plot**: Sera's storyline hub. Team Cassian's escape route. The truth is broadcast from here.
- **First Appearance**: Arc 2 Ch 7 (Sera subplot) / Arc 3 Ch 11 (Sera POV — first direct appearance).
- **Last Appearance**: Arc 3 Ch 15 — After the broadcast, the Capital is in chaos. Ministry control fractures.
- **Key Events**:
  - Sera discovers Nio's file (Arc 2 Ch 7 — subplot)
  - Sera's decision to leak documents (Ch 11)
  - Team Cassian's infiltration of the steam pipelines (Ch 13)
  - Sera's truth broadcast (Ch 15)
- **Final State**: Remains standing but loses power after the Sky-Dam collapses. Becomes a historical site in the new world.

### 4. The Salt Ward
- **Type**: Underground detention and experimentation facility
- **Geography**: Built directly beneath the Sky-Dam Engine. Its deepest chambers connect to the Sky-Dam's foundation — water from the Sky Ocean seeps through the walls. The entire facility hums with the Engine's pulse.
- **Relative Position**: Directly under the Sky-Dam. Accessible from the Capital via underground tunnels or from mountain passes used by The Chorus.
- **Who Controls**: Ministry — The Harvesters division. Doctor Aldis Prahm was chief researcher (until killed by Varek).
- **Importance to Plot**: Where Nio was held for 10 years. Where Team Cael rescues him. Site of the Party Split. Site of Odran's flashback confession.
- **First Appearance**: Arc 3 Ch 12
- **Last Appearance**: Arc 3 Ch 14 — Abandoned after the jailbreak. Later collapses as the Sky-Dam falls.
- **Key Events**:
  - Nio rescued (Ch 12)
  - Odran's flashback: Inquisitor weeping in Nio's cell (Ch 12)
  - Party Split (Ch 12)
  - Wren discovered — her Leviathan drawings (Ch 12)
- **Final State**: Destroyed. Buried under the Sky-Dam's collapse. The bodies of the Harvesters' victims remain — some of them, like Lienn, never recovered.

### 5. The Mistwood (ป่าหมอก)
- **Type**: Reclaimed wilderness — Brine-touched refuge
- **Geography**: A forest where trees draw moisture from the fog. Leaves are fleshy and rib-like — they breathe in unison during humid nights. The ground is soft — damp with standing water. Pale-Moths drift through the mist in clouds. Visibility is low. Sound travels strangely — as if the forest amplifies the Leviathan's frequency.
- **Relative Position**: South of the Sky-Dam. Between the Salt Ward and Pearl Colony. ~3-4 days on foot.
- **Who Controls**: No one — but The Chorus (escaped brine-touched led by the Voice-Mother) live here. They are not hostile but will kill trespassers who threaten them.
- **Importance to Plot**: Team Cael's escape route after the Party Split. Odran's psychological collapse. First meeting with the Voice-Mother.
- **First Appearance**: Arc 3 Ch 14
- **Last Appearance**: Arc 4 Ch 17 — Team exits toward Pearl Colony. The forest remains. After the ocean falls, parts of the Mistwood survive as the new world's mangrove swamps.
- **Key Events**:
  - Odran collapses from guilt — confesses to Cael (Ch 14)
  - Voice-Mother's test: Cael must not flinch at her touch (Ch 14-15)
  - Varek's pursuit through the canopy (Ch 15)
- **Final State**: Survives in altered form. The fog-tolerant trees form the basis of the new coastal ecosystem.

---

### 6. Pearl Colony
- **Type**: Colony / Sanatorium for brine-touched
- **Geography**: A village beside a grey lake, south of the Mistwood. The buildings are pale — built from coral and shell. The Warden's "garden" is a courtyard filled with fully calcified brine-touched — human-shaped coral statues, still alive, still watching.
- **Relative Position**: South of the Mistwood. ~3-4 days walk from the Salt Ward. ~2 days walk from Bone Harbor.
- **Who Controls**: The Warden of Pearl — independent. Not Ministry, but not benevolent either.
- **Importance to Plot**: Reunion point — Team Cael and Team Cassian meet here. Varek defects here. Kaelen Mor's warning. Body horror centerpiece.
- **First Appearance**: Arc 4 Ch 16
- **Last Appearance**: Arc 4 Ch 17 — Team flees. The Colony is later flooded when the ocean falls.
- **Key Events**:
  - Team reunion. Tovin and Maera's silent reunion (Ch 16)
  - The Warden's "garden" revealed — living coral statues (Ch 16)
  - Kaelen Mor's warning to Cael (Ch 17)
  - Team escapes the Warden (Ch 17)
- **Final State**: Destroyed by water. The Warden's fate unknown. The coral statues — still alive — now rest at the bottom of the new sea.

---

### 7. Bone Harbor
- **Type**: Abandoned shipyard
- **Geography**: An ancient shipyard buried in dry mud. When rain falls, the dried hulls begin to float out of the dirt — remembering their past. Located at the southernmost point of the known world. Keening Crabs fill the harbor — their crying is deafening at night.
- **Relative Position**: South of Pearl Colony. ~2 days walk. Southernmost accessible location before the Sky-Dam approach.
- **Who Controls**: No one — abandoned. Pip is the unofficial dock-keeper.
- **Importance to Plot**: Launch point for the Sky-Dam mission. Loran's departure. Cassian kills Sergeant Durn here.
- **First Appearance**: Arc 4 Ch 19
- **Last Appearance**: Arc 4 Ch 20 — The *Albatross* departs into the storm.
- **Key Events**:
  - Loran calculates fatality vector — 1.2% survival (Ch 19)
  - Loran departs — gives Ilyra his charts (Ch 19)
  - Pip meets the crew (Ch 19)
  - Cassian kills Sergeant Durn (Ch 20)
  - Cael's speech before departure (Ch 20)
- **Final State**: The harbor is submerged when the ocean falls. The dried hulls finally float for real. The Keening Crabs' crying stops — fewer human remains to feed on.

### 8. The Sky-Dam Engine
- **Type**: Colossal structure — Machine / organism hybrid
- **Geography**: The highest point of the Sky Ocean. Built from bone, shell, and copper by the Ascendant Engineers. Inside, pipes beat like blood vessels. Walls are inscribed with the names of all 73 Ascendant Engineers. The Regulator Chamber contains the Tide-Key Engine — the device Cael bonds with. The Dead Parliament's consciousness archive is embedded in the walls.
- **Relative Position**: Above everything. Accessible only by airship from Bone Harbor — ~1 day at maximum speed through worsening storm conditions.
- **Who Controls**: Dead Parliament (automated) + Marrow Saint (occupying force).
- **Importance to Plot**: Final confrontation with the Dead Parliament and Marrow Saint. Cael becomes the Tide-Key. The Sky-Dam's collapse begins here.
- **First Appearance**: Arc 5 Ch 21 (exterior) / Ch 22 (interior)
- **Last Appearance**: Arc 7 Ch 32 — Collapses. Cael dissolves into the Engine. Marrow dies alone.
- **Key Events**:
  - Maera breaches the exterior defenses (Ch 21)
  - Maera returns Tovin's repaired knife (Ch 22)
  - Dead Parliament contacts Nio (Ch 22)
  - Nio rejects the cure — stabs his own chest (Ch 23)
  - Marrow Saint confronts Cael in the name room (Ch 24)
  - Cael bonds with the Tide-Key Engine (Ch 25, 32)
- **Final State**: Destroyed. The Engine's collapse releases the ocean. The Dead Parliament's consciousness is extinguished. Cael's consciousness remains — dissolved into the rain.

### 9. Nacre-Vault
- **Type**: Wealthy merchant city
- **Geography**: Built inside natural marble caves west of the Sky-Dam. The city's architecture is underground — carved into the limestone. Rich families live in the upper caves. Workers live in the lower tunnels near the "pearl factories."
- **Relative Position**: West of the Sky-Dam. Sister city to the Capital — connected by trade routes.
- **Who Controls**: Merchant families who profit from "pearls" harvested from brine-touched corpses. The Ministry has a presence but the merchants hold real power.
- **Importance to Plot**: The city that resists evacuation because its economy depends on the Sky-Dam. Maera discovers the pearl factory.
- **First Appearance**: Arc 6 Ch 27
- **Last Appearance**: Arc 6 Ch 27
- **Key Events**:

  - Varek kills Master Harrow — *"Unimportant people don't deserve speeches."* (Ch 27)
  - Half the population evacuates — half stays (Ch 27)
- **Final State**: Flooded. The merchants who stayed drown with their pearls. The caves become underwater ruins.

### 10. Hallowmere
- **Type**: Religious city
- **Geography**: Built on the shore of a sacred lake west of the Sky-Dam. The lake is believed to be a "tear of God." The city's architecture is white stone — temples, shrines, the Gate of Absolution (the gate mechanism Odran cranks).
- **Relative Position**: West of the Sky-Dam. Connected to Nacre-Vault by pilgrimage roads.
- **Who Controls**: Bishop Corvein (religious) + Ministry (political — light presence).
- **Importance to Plot**: The hardest city to evacuate. Bishop Corvein's resistance. Odran's sacrifice.
- **First Appearance**: Arc 6 Ch 29
- **Last Appearance**: Arc 6 Ch 30 — Odran dies here.
- **Key Events**:

  - Odran cranks the wheel — fingers crack — recites 73 names — drowns (Ch 30)

### 11. Southern Valleys (หุบเขาใต้)
- **Type**: Safe zone — Loran's home
- **Geography**: Valleys south of Bone Harbor. Far from the Descent Zone. Mild climate. Fertile soil not yet saturated with salt. Small independent communities — no Ministry presence.
- **Relative Position**: South of Bone Harbor. Several days' travel on foot.
- **Who Controls**: No one — independent communities.
- **Importance to Plot**: Where Loran returns to save his family. Where Yara Thorne grows up. The basis of the new civilization in the Aftermath.
- **First Appearance**: Arc 4 Ch 19 (Loran departs for here)
- **Last Appearance**: Arc 7 Ch 35 (Epilogue — Thorne family lineage, 7 generations)
- **Key Events**: Loran successfully evacuates his family before the flood. The Southern Valleys become the nucleus of post-flood civilization.

## PART 4: HORROR ZONES

---

### 14. The Upside Reef
- **Type**: Inverted coral formation
- **Geography**: A massive coral reef hanging downward from the bottom of the Sky Ocean. Corals grow toward the earth. "Eats" birds, airships, and — according to folk belief — the memories of travelers who pass too close.
- **Relative Position**: Under the Sky Ocean — between Capital and the Sky-Dam approach route.
- **Importance to Plot**: Navigational hazard. The *Albatross* must fly through a gap in the reef. Atmospheric horror.
- **First Appearance**: Arc 2 Ch 8 — Mentioned. Arc 2 Ch 9 — Seen from a distance.
- **Final State**: Falls with the ocean. The coral that hung upside down for 1,000 years finally touches the earth right-side up.

---

### 15. The Corpse Fields (สุสานลอยฟ้า)
- **Type**: Floating graveyard
- **Geography**: An area of the Sky Ocean where corpses from sunken cities accumulate. They do not rot — preserved by the cold deep. Eyes open. Mouths move as if still speaking. Pale-Moths swarm here constantly. The Drowned Choir is loudest here.
- **Relative Position**: In the Sky Ocean — coordinates known to salvage families like Ilyra's.
- **Importance to Plot**: Ilyra's family worked here. Henrick "Rust-Nail" died here. Ilyra visits in Arc 5 before the Sky-Dam mission.
- **First Mention**: Arc 1 Ch 3 — Ilyra's backstory.
- **Appears**: Arc 5 (before Ch 21) — Ilyra's detour.
- **Final State**: Falls to earth. The preserved corpses finally find rest on the seabed they died above.

## GEOGRAPHICAL LOGIC (เหตุผลที่ตั้งของแต่ละเมือง)

| City | Reason for Location |
|---|---|
| **Elaris** | Built on the mountain because the lowlands were old seabed — unsafe, prone to salt saturation. The mountain was the only stable ground after the ocean was lifted. |
| **Capital** | Built on the old continental shelf — flat, defensible plateau. Close to the Sky-Dam's energy output — the Ministry's power depends on proximity to the Engine. |
| **Salt Ward** | Built directly under the Sky-Dam to use the Engine's water pressure for experiments. The Harvesters needed access to Sky Ocean water for their research. |
| **Nacre-Vault** | Built in natural caves because the cave system contained brine-touched fossils from the first generation of mutation — the "pearl" industry started with mining these fossils. |
| **Hallowmere** | Built on the sacred lake shore because the lake was the first place where rain from the Sky Ocean collected naturally — seen as a divine sign. |
| **Pearl Colony** | Built beside the salt lake because the high salinity accelerates the calcification process — the Warden chose this location for his "garden." |
| **Bone Harbor** | Built at the southernmost point because it was the last shipyard before the Sky-Dam was completed — the Engineers used it as a supply depot. Abandoned when the ocean was lifted. |
| **Southern Valleys** | Far enough south to be outside the Descent Zone. Fertile soil not yet contaminated by bitter rain. Chosen by Loran and other refugees for its survivability. |
