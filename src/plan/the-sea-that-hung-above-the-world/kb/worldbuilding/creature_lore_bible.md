# 🐋 Creature & Disease Bible: The Sea That Hung Above The World

This document catalogs all mutated creatures, diseases, conditions, and ecological phenomena in the world. Every entry has a biological basis connected to the Sky Ocean ecology and a narrative purpose in the story. Nothing exists "just to be cool."

---

## PART 1: CREATURES (11 Species)

---

### 1. Cloud Whales (วาฬเมฆ)
- **Type**: Genetically altered cetacean — Mammal
- **Appearance**: 40-80 meters long. Grey-blue skin with bioluminescent scarring along old wounds. Fins disproportionately wide — adapted to "swim" through the dense air of the Sky Ocean. Iron hooks and metal fittings still embedded in their bones from when the Ascendant Engineers anchored them as supports for the Sky-Dam.
- **Behavior**: Migrate along Sky Ocean wind currents. Sing low-frequency songs that humans perceive as vibration. Repeat the same songs over centuries — believed to be "memories" of the ocean before it was lifted.
- **Origin/Lore**: Ancient humans captured Cloud Whales and used them as living pillars to support the Sky-Dam. The iron hooks in their bones are the remnants. When the Sky-Dam falls, the Cloud Whales descend with the ocean — swimming down through the Descent Zone.
- **First Appearance**: Arc 2 Ch 6 — Cael's first glimpse of the Sky Ocean. A pod of Cloud Whales migrating through the Shroud.
- **Last Appearance**: Arc 7 Ch 33 — Cloud Whales descending with the falling ocean.
- **Danger Level**: Low — Not aggressive. But collision with an airship is catastrophic.
- **Survival Method**: Yield right of way. Show respect. Do not make sudden noise.
- **Effect on Humans**: Their songs can trigger buried memories in brine-touched. Prolonged exposure without mutation causes Rain-Madness.
- **Narrative Use**: Atmospheric majesty. Omen — when Cloud Whales begin descending lower than usual, it means the ocean is about to fall.

### 2. Reef-Hounds (หมาปะการัง)
- **Type**: Mutated mammal — Canine origin
- **Appearance**: Wolf-like body, 1.2-1.5 meters at the shoulder. Skull has burst open — red coral grows through the bone, forming a jagged external helmet. Teeth are sharp coral fragments, constantly regenerating. Eyes are milky white — no iris. Paws leave salt-crust footprints.
- **Behavior**: Hunt in packs of 3-12. Communicate by rhythmically tapping the coral on their heads — a clicking language. Retain canine instincts — "guard doorways." Will circle the ruins of houses and sit beside broken doors, waiting for owners who will never return.
- **Origin/Lore**: Domestic dogs abandoned in flooded cities. Rain transformed them. The coral growth is the earth's memory of the seabed reclaiming their bodies.
- **First Appearance**: Arc 2 Ch 7 — Ambush on the *Albatross* deck among the drowned ruins.
- **Last Appearance**: Arc 7 Ch 31 — Final swarm during the Hallowmere evacuation, slaughtered by Varek.
- **Danger Level**: HIGH — One is manageable. A pack is a massacre.
- **Survival Method**: Never let them surround you. Target the lead hound — the pack's coordination breaks without it.
- **Effect on Humans**: Bites introduce coral spores. Wounds begin calcifying within hours if untreated.
- **Narrative Use**: Ambush tension. Chase scenes. Evacuation combat. Emotional horror — the dogs still guard houses with no houses left.

### 3. Pale-Moths (ผีเสื้อซีด)
- **Type**: Mutated insect — Lepidopteran origin
- **Appearance**: Wingspan of a human palm. Wings are translucent like lung tissue — blue bioluminescent veins visible through the membrane. Flight is completely silent. Body is a pale, powdered white — like ash that has not yet settled.
- **Behavior**: Land on the mouth or nose of the dying. Do not eat flesh — they consume the "final breath." Gather in swarms around battlefields and disaster sites hours before death occurs.
- **Origin/Lore**: Born from droplets of vaporized seawater falling on corpses. Folk belief says a Pale-Moth carries the memory of the dead person in its wings.
- **First Appearance**: Arc 1 Ch 4 — The village the Ministry burned. Pale-Moths swarm the pyre.
- **Last Appearance**: Arc 6 Ch 30 — Landing on Odran's lips as he drowns in Hallowmere's gate room.
- **Danger Level**: None to the living — But their presence is the most reliable death omen in the world.
- **Survival Method**: You cannot hide from them. If Pale-Moths land on you, you are already dying — you just don't know it yet.
- **Effect on Humans**: Psychological. Seeing a Pale-Moth land on someone you love is worse than watching them die.
- **Narrative Use**: Death omen. Visual horror. Emotional cue — when a Pale-Moth appears, someone is about to die. Used most powerfully on Odran's final scene.

### 4. Glass Eels (ปลาไหลแก้ว)
- **Type**: Parasite / Messenger organism
- **Appearance**: 5-15 cm long. Body completely transparent — internal organs visible as blue bioluminescent threads. Swim through water and through the bloodstream of brine-touched with equal ease.
- **Behavior**: Enter the human body through open wounds or by drinking seawater. Navigate through veins toward the heart. The closer they get to the heart, the clearer the host hears the sea's voice — specifically, the Leviathan's frequency.
- **Origin/Lore**: Unknown whether created by the Ascendant Engineers or emerged naturally from the Sky Ocean. Function as "transmitters" for the Leviathan's signal to brine-touched hosts.
- **First Appearance**: Arc 1 Ch 4 — In the bottled house Cael finds. Tiny Glass Eels swim inside the miniature rooms.
- **Last Appearance**: Arc 6 Ch 30 — Swimming past Odran's ankles in the flooding gate room.
- **Danger Level**: Not directly lethal — But they accelerate the Leviathan's call. Makes the host's mutation progress faster.
- **Survival Method**: Do not drink Sky Ocean water. Treat all wounds immediately with salt-free water.
- **Effect on Humans**: Increased Leviathan frequency reception. Accelerated brine-touch mutation. In late stages, hosts can "hear" other brine-touched across distances.
- **Narrative Use**: Body horror — eels visible in veins. Communication mechanism — Nio uses them to sense other brine-touched. Connection to Leviathan.

### 5. Keening Crabs (ปูคร่ำครวญ)
- **Type**: Mutated crustacean
- **Appearance**: 30-50 cm shell diameter. Shell is bone-white, textured like a human skull. Each crab has a sound-chamber on its shell — when vibrated, produces a sound indistinguishable from a crying child. Thousands together produce a chorus of weeping.
- **Behavior**: Congregate by the thousands on shipwrecks and drowned ruins. Their crying intensifies when humans approach. The frequency of crying matches the frequency of the last human to die in that location.
- **Origin/Lore**: Common shore crabs that fed on human remains in flooded areas. The crying sound is a side effect of chemical compounds in human tissue interacting with their shell chambers.
- **First Appearance**: Arc 4 Ch 20 — Bone Harbor at night. The entire harbor cries as the *Albatross* prepares to depart.
- **Last Appearance**: Arc 7 Ch 35 — New shoreline after the ocean falls. The crying is fainter now — fewer human remains to feed on.
- **Danger Level**: Low — don't bite. But the psychological pressure of their crying is immense.
- **Survival Method**: Ear protection. Move through quickly. Do not linger.
- **Effect on Humans**: Psychological. Prolonged exposure causes auditory hallucinations — hearing the voices of dead loved ones in the crab-song.
- **Narrative Use**: Sound horror. Atmospheric tension. The Bone Harbor departure scene — the crew silently preparing for the suicide mission while the harbor cries like children.

### 6. Hull-Barnacles (เพรียงกินเรือ)
- **Type**: Parasitic structure organism
- **Appearance**: 10-30 cm shell diameter. Black, glossy shells. Long white tendrils extend from the shell opening. Release green bioluminescent spores into the air. Sharp enough to cut rope and thin metal.
- **Behavior**: Attach to the underside of airships. Consume wood and metal — slowly. Release hallucinogenic spores that cause crew members to see things that aren't there.
- **Origin/Lore**: Evolved in the Sky Ocean where shipwrecks are abundant. The hallucinogenic spores are a defense mechanism — disoriented crew means the barnacles feed undisturbed.
- **First Appearance**: Arc 2 Ch 6 — Maera discovers something attached to the hull with "teeth."
- **Last Appearance**: Arc 5 Ch 21 — Maera scrapes off the last barnacles before the Sky-Dam approach.
- **Danger Level**: Medium — If untreated, the ship falls apart. Spores cause hallucinations.
- **Survival Method**: Regular hull scraping. Gas masks during scraping.
- **Effect on Humans**: Spore inhalation causes hallucinations — usually of the person you most fear losing. Maera sees Tovin dead. Ilyra sees her father's corpse ship.
- **Narrative Use**: Resource tension — the ship is deteriorating. Hallucination scenes — characters forced to confront their deepest fears. Reveals character through what they hallucinate.

### 7. Shade-Flame (แสงเงา)
- **Type**: Bio-luminescent phenomenon — Possibly sentient?
- **Appearance**: Soft blue-white lights floating in low mist. No fixed shape. Move slowly — like someone walking through shadow. When approached, they retreat. When ignored, they follow.
- **Behavior**: Appear in areas where brine-touched have died. Folk belief: they are "souls" or "residual memories." Scientists (Sera): chemical reaction between decomposition gases and the water memory of the Sky Ocean. The truth is deliberately ambiguous.
- **Origin/Lore**: Unknown. Perhaps nothing more than a chemical reaction. Perhaps exactly what the folk believe.
- **First Appearance**: Arc 1 Ch 4 — The burned village. Shade-Flames drifting through the smoke.
- **Last Appearance**: Arc 6 Ch 29 — Hallowmere. Shade-Flames gathering above the sacred lake as Bishop Corvein's followers drown.
- **Danger Level**: None. But they haunt the mind.
- **Survival Method**: Ignore them. They cannot harm you. They will not stop following until you leave the area.
- **Effect on Humans**: Psychological. People who see Shade-Flame report feeling "watched" for days afterward. Brine-touched can sometimes hear faint voices from them.
- **Narrative Use**: Atmospheric horror. Visual beauty masking dread. Ambiguity — is the supernatural real in this world, or is it just chemistry?

### 8. Spine Corals (ปะการังกระดูกสันหลัง)
- **Type**: Parasitic coral — Calcification organism
- **Appearance**: Pale white coral that attaches to open wounds. Grows along the spine, extracting calcium from vertebrae. When fully mature, bursts through the skin in a "bloom" of coral flowers. The host is fully conscious but completely immobile.
- **Behavior**: Infects brine-touched who stay too long in the Pearl Colony. The Warden of Pearl "cultivates" it — he considers the final coral bloom a form of beauty.
- **Origin/Lore**: A side effect of the Warden's "treatment" for brine-touched. He claims it preserves them. The reality: it turns them into living statues.
- **First Appearance**: Arc 4 Ch 16 — The Warden's "garden." Rows of human-shaped coral. Eyes still tracking movement.
- **Last Appearance**: Arc 4 Ch 17 — The team flees. The garden remains.
- **Danger Level**: Slow death — takes months. The host is conscious the entire time.
- **Survival Method**: Do not accept the Warden's "treatment."
- **Effect on Humans**: Complete immobilization. Consciousness preserved. Unable to speak, move, or close eyes. A living death.
- **Narrative Use**: Body horror centerpiece of Arc 4. The Warden's "garden" is the visual culmination of the theme: "nature reclaiming the human body is beautiful and horrifying simultaneously."

### 9. The Drowned Choir (คณะนักร้องใต้ทะเล)
- **Type**: Phenomenon — Possibly a collective organism? Possibly a natural echo effect?
- **Appearance**: No physical form. A chorus of hundreds of human voices singing the same song. Comes from beneath the surface of the Sky Ocean. The song changes depending on how many people have recently died in the water.
- **Behavior**: Grows louder when someone is dying in the Sky Ocean. Sings the same melody that the dying person last heard or sang in life. The song propagates through water — even through pipes and rain.
- **Origin/Lore**: Sera's theory: *"It is the collective echo of nervous systems still active in dead brine-touched bodies at the bottom of the Sky Ocean. They sing what they remember."* The folk belief: *"The dead are singing to call the living home."*
- **First Appearance**: Arc 2 Ch 7 — Faintly, as the *Albatross* sails over a drowned city.
- **Last Appearance**: Arc 7 Ch 33 — Loudest. The Sky-Dam has collapsed. Thousands are dying. The Choir is deafening.
- **Danger Level**: Listening too long causes dissociation — not an attack, but a "perception" the human mind cannot withstand.
- **Survival Method**: Do not listen. Plug ears. Sing a different song — it confuses the Choir momentarily.
- **Effect on Humans**: Prolonged listening causes temporary loss of self — the listener begins to "remember" lives that are not their own.
- **Narrative Use**: Sound horror. Thematic resonance — all the dead have a "voice." The Choir is the cumulative sound of all the world's drowned.

### 10. The First Leviathan (มังกรทะเลแรก)
- **Type**: Unclassifiable — Possibly an animal. Possibly a nervous system. Possibly a god. Possibly the wound of the world given sentience.
- **Appearance**: No one has seen it in its entirety. Those who have glimpsed parts describe: *"Like seeing the brain of God… or the boundary where the world connects to something else."* Nio — the only one who has truly seen it — cannot describe it in human language. He can only transmit whale frequencies of the experience.
- **Behavior**: Does not "do" anything. It "is." It is the total perception of the entire ancient ocean — every death, every birth, every current, every memory of the seabed. Its existence makes all sea-life in the Sky Ocean begin to "remember." It does not want the ocean to fall. It does not hate humanity. It simply feels the pain of the ocean being torn from the earth, and that feeling is loud enough to drive humans insane.
- **Origin/Lore**: May be the first living organism of the primordial ocean, lifted into the sky when the ocean was raised 1,000 years ago. Or it may be a "product" of the ocean being torn — the wound itself, alive.
- **First Appearance (indirect)**: Arc 2 Ch 6 — Cael feels "a silence that isn't silence" beneath the waves.
- **First Appearance (direct)**: Arc 5 Ch 25 — Nio sees it fully. His human language begins to fail.
- **Last Appearance**: Arc 7 Ch 34 — Returns to the sea as the ocean falls. Disperses — its consciousness spreads across the new ocean, no longer concentrated in one crushing point.
- **Danger Level**: Beyond measurement — Does not "attack," but humans who look at it directly "understand" the universe too completely.
- **Survival Method**: Do not look. Do not listen. Do not attempt to understand. Human consciousness cannot contain what the Leviathan perceives.
- **Effect on Humans**: Direct sight → irreversible perceptual overload. The viewer understands the entire ecological system — every death, every current, every memory — and the human mind breaks trying to contain it.
- **Narrative Use**: Climax. Cael must "negotiate" with it through Nio. It is not an antagonist — it is a natural force that must be "released" back to the earth. The Leviathan is the ultimate expression of the theme: nature is not evil, but it is not gentle.

### 10-A. The Choir-Warden (ผู้พิทักษ์คณะนักร้อง) — Layer 3/ Sky-Dam Threshold
- **Type**: Collective consciousness organism — Guardian entity
- **Appearance**: No fixed physical form. The Choir-Warden manifests as a shifting mass of translucent humanoid figures — faces, hands, torsos — that emerge from and dissolve back into a central core of dense, salt-heavy water. The figures are the bodies of the Dead Parliament's victims: Brine-Spore hosts who fully transformed and whose consciousnesses were absorbed into the Sky-Dam's structure instead of being released. Hundreds of them. Their faces are recognizable — some wear Salt Ward uniforms, some wear the clothes of drowned sailors, some are children. Their mouths move in unison, but the sound is not a scream — it is the Drowned Choir, given physical form. When the Warden is at rest, it fills the corridor leading to the Regulator Chamber like a wall of living water. When it moves, the figures reach out — not to attack, but to touch. To feel if the approaching person is "one of them" — a host, a brine-touched, someone who shares the parasite's frequency.
- **Behavior**: The Choir-Warden does not attack. It is a guardian — created by the Dead Parliament from the harvested consciousnesses of failed experiments over a thousand years. Its purpose is to prevent anyone from reaching the Regulator Chamber who does not share the Brine-Spore frequency — anyone who is not "of the sea." It tests intruders by reaching into their minds through physical contact. The hundreds of consciousnesses within it ask one question, in unison, through the Drowned Choir's song: *"Are you the sea, or are you the thief?"* If the answer — felt, not spoken — is "thief," the Warden closes the path. The water solidifies into an impenetrable barrier. The figures become hostile. If the answer is "sea" — if the intruder is a brine-touched host, or if a non-host has been accepted by one — the Warden parts. The figures recede. The path opens. The Warden does not judge morality. It judges connection. It is the ultimate expression of the Dead Parliament's logic: only those who have paid the price of transformation may pass.
- **Origin/Lore**: The Dead Parliament did not intend to create the Choir-Warden. It is a byproduct — the accumulated consciousness-residue of every Brine-Spore host who fully transformed while connected to the Sky-Dam's systems. Their bodies were consumed. Their minds were archived. Over centuries, those archived minds merged into a single collective awareness — not human, not machine, not Leviathan. Something between all three. The Parliament learned to direct it — to use it as a guardian. But they cannot control what it asks. The question — *"Are you the sea, or are you the thief?"* — is not the Parliament's question. It is the question the dead have been asking for a thousand years.
- **Foreshadowing — What the Heroes Have Experienced**:
  - **Arc 2 Ch 7**: The first time the Drowned Choir is heard. Cael feels something beneath the singing — *"like someone was listening to us, not just singing."* Ilyra dismisses it as nerves. Cassian says nothing but keeps his hand on his weapon.
  - **Arc 3 Ch 14**: In the Mistwood, Odran hears the Choir through the pipes — louder than Cael has ever heard it. He freezes. *"They're asking something… I can almost hear the words."* When Cael asks what they're asking, Odran cannot answer. His guilt is too loud.
  - **Arc 4 Ch 20**: At Bone Harbor, the Keening Crabs' crying synchronizes with the Choir for one moment — every sound in the harbor aligning into a single frequency. Every member of the crew feels it. Tovin: *"Something down there… just looked at us."* Loran marks it on his charts: "Choir Anomaly — DO NOT APPROACH."
- **First Direct Appearance**: Arc 5 Ch 22 — After Nio contacts the Dead Parliament, the corridor ahead fills with the Choir-Warden. The Drowned Choir — which the crew has heard as distant singing for three arcs — is now in front of them. Physical. Hundreds of faces in the water. The question fills the corridor: *"Are you the sea, or are you the thief?"*
- **Last Appearance**: Arc 5 Ch 22 — The Warden parts after Nio steps forward and touches the water. The faces recognize him — he is a host, one of them. The path to the Regulator Chamber opens. The Warden does not appear again. When the Sky-Dam collapses, the consciousnesses within it are released — they become part of the Drowned Choir that sings at the ocean's fall.
- **Danger Level**: IMPASSABLE to non-hosts. The Warden cannot be fought. Weapons pass through it. The water reforms. The only way past is to be accepted.
- **Survival Method**: Be brine-touched — or be accompanied by someone who is. The Warden accepts Nio. Because Nio accepts the crew, the Warden accepts the crew. It is not a test of worthiness. It is a test of belonging.
- **Effect on Humans**: The Warden's touch is not physically harmful, but psychologically devastating. Each figure that touches you transfers a fragment of its death-memory — a flash of the Salt Ward, a sensation of drowning, a child's last thought. Non-hosts who are touched by too many figures experience temporary ego dissolution — they forget who they are for several minutes. Odran is touched by 73 figures — one for each child he sent to the Salt Ward. He experiences every death. He does not speak for an hour afterward.
- **Narrative Use**: The final gatekeeper before the Dead Parliament. Transforms the Drowned Choir from atmospheric horror into an active, intelligent presence. Reveals that the Dead Parliament did not just experiment on brine-touched — they harvested them. The Choir-Warden is the proof. It also provides the crucial emotional beat: Nio — the brother Cael has been trying to "save" — is the one who saves them. His infection is not a weakness. It is the key.

## PART 2: PARASITES & CONDITIONS (ปรสิตและสภาวะ)

---

### 1. Brine-Spores (สปอร์น้ำเค็ม) — The Core Parasite
- **Type**: Parasitic microorganism — Spore-form
- **Life Cycle**: Brine-Spores are carried in Sky Ocean rain. On contact with human skin, the spore burrows through the epidermis within minutes — the entry point becomes translucent as the spore dissolves the skin's pigment layer to access the bloodstream. Once in the blood, it migrates toward the nervous system, following bioelectric signals toward the brainstem. The migration takes weeks to months (Stage 1-2). Upon reaching the central nervous system, the spore begins integrating with the host's neural architecture — this is the point of no return (Stage 3). The parasite does not kill the host. It transforms the host into a hybrid organism capable of surviving in the sea. Nio is still Nio — he just experiences reality through a biology that is no longer exclusively human.
- **Early Stage Symptoms (Week 1-4)**: Entry point becomes translucent — veins and muscle visible beneath. Cold sensation that never fades. The parasite is consuming pigment cells to build its own protective membrane.
- **Mid Stage Symptoms (Month 2-6)**: Pale scales form — the parasite constructs external armor from the host's mineral intake. Bones soften or harden — the parasite restructures the skeleton for pressure adaptation. Constant water sound in the head — the parasite has reached the inner ear, calibrating hearing to Leviathan frequencies.
- **Late Stage Symptoms (Month 6+)**: Full semi-aquatic transformation — functional gills (parasite-constructed respiratory tissue), coral-reinforced bones, webbing, fins. Speech degrades into whale frequencies — the parasite has rewired the language centers. The parasite coexists with the host's consciousness — the host is still themselves, just experiencing reality through a new biology.
- **First Mention**: Arc 1 Ch 1 — Folk tales describe "the shimmer in the rain."
- **First Appearance**: Arc 1 Ch 2 (Flashback) — Nio's fingers turn translucent. Under magnification: microscopic spore entry wounds.
- **Last Appearance**: Arc 7 Ch 33 — Nio and Varek complete transformation.
- **Plot Impact**: Central conflict driver. The reason the Ministry exists (containment). The reason Cael searches for Nio. The reason Varek hunts and later defects.
- **Character Impact**: Cael (fear of his own infection), Nio (10-year host), Varek (hiding infection), Odran (guilt over sending children to be "studied").
- **Narrative Use**: Body horror of parasitic invasion. Thematic question: if a parasite transforms you into something that can survive — is it a disease or an adaptation?

---

### 2. Ash-Lung (ปอดเถ้า) — Environmental Condition
- **Type**: Environmental — not parasitic. Long-term inhalation of airborne ash and vaporized sea minerals from Ministry burn-sites and Sky Ocean spray.
- **Symptoms**: Audible wet rattle in the chest when breathing. Coughing up grey-flecked phlegm. Lungs slowly fill with fluid — the patient drowns on dry land.
- **First Appearance**: Arc 3 Ch 13 — Capital workers in the steam-pipe corridors. Odran hears the "water in their lungs" as they pass.
- **Narrative Use**: Background horror. Odran's guilt trigger — he hears Ash-Lung and remembers the Salt Ward. A reminder that even those not directly touched by rain are dying from the Sky Ocean.

---

### 3. Coral-Spores (สปอร์ปะการัง) — Parasitic Coral
- **Type**: Parasitic coral spore — Calcification organism
- **Life Cycle**: Coral-Spores enter through open wounds or are deliberately introduced (the Warden's "treatment"). The spore attaches to the nearest bone and begins extracting calcium, consuming the host's skeleton cell by cell and rebuilding it as living coral. The process takes months. The host is conscious throughout. The parasite preserves the nervous system — the host's bioelectric field nourishes the coral. Fully matured colonies bloom from the host's body like flowering coral. The Warden considers this the highest form of beauty.
- **Symptoms**: Small coral buds break through skin at fingertips. Spread inward along bone lines toward the spine. Terminal stage: entire skeleton replaced by coral. Host immobile but fully conscious. Eyes track movement. The colony pulses faintly — bioluminescent in the dark.
- **Origin**: Naturally occurring in Pearl Colony's salt-saturated environment. The Warden cultivates and refines the spores.
- **First Appearance**: Arc 4 Ch 16 — Pearl Colony. The Warden's "garden" — rows of Coral-Spore hosts in full bloom.
- **Narrative Use**: Body horror centerpiece of Arc 4. The "living statues." The parasite makes the host "beautiful" — and that IS the horror.

---

### 4. The Silence (ความเงียบ)
- **Type**: Neurological condition — Late-stage Brine-Spore infection
- **Cause**: Brine-Spore hosts who have connected with the Leviathan for too long.
- **Symptoms**: Gradual loss of ability to hear human speech. Can only hear the frequencies of the sea. Cannot speak human language — can only produce whale sounds. Memory loss — human words disappear one by one. The first word lost is always the name of the person they love most.
- **First Appearance**: Arc 5 Ch 25 — Nio begins losing language. Forgets the word "water." Can still say "brother."
- **Narrative Use**: The emotional core of Nio's tragedy. His final scene: he has forgotten the word "brother" but sings Cael's childhood melody in whale frequency. Cael understands. The loss of language is the loss of the last thread connecting Nio to his human life.

---

### 5. The Warden's Bloom (ดอกวอร์เดน) — Parasitic Calcification Agent
- **Type**: Parasitic organism — Full-tissue calcification agent, cultivated by the Warden of Pearl
- **Life Cycle**: The Warden's Bloom is an engineered parasite administered via injection or salve to brine-touched who come seeking his "mercy." Once in the bloodstream, it converts all soft tissue — muscle, skin, organs — into a unified calcium-carbonate structure over weeks to months. The parasite feeds on the host's metabolic energy to fuel the calcification. Unlike Coral-Spores (which replace bone), the Warden's Bloom converts EVERYTHING into a single living statue. The nervous system is conserved — the host is conscious, trapped in an immobile body, for years. Some hosts in the Warden's garden are decades old. Their eyes still follow visitors.
- **Symptoms**: Body hardens from feet upward. Skin becomes marble-white, smooth, cool. Cannot move, speak, or close eyes. Consciousness preserved.
- **Origin**: The Warden discovered the organism in the Pearl Colony lake. He cultivates it. He calls it a "blessing of preservation." It is not.
- **First Appearance**: Arc 4 Ch 16 — Pearl Colony. The Warden's "garden."
- **Narrative Use**: The full horror of "preservation" as violation. The parasite does not kill — it collects.

---

### 6. Rain-Madness (บ้าฝน)
- **Type**: Psychological condition
- **Cause**: Non-mutated humans who hear the Leviathan's call — typically occurs in Rain Listeners who refuse their role. The Leviathan's frequency is too loud for an unmutated mind.
- **Symptoms**: Hallucinations of the ocean in dry places. Speaking a language no one understands. Compulsion to walk toward the Sky Ocean. Terminal stage: the afflicted walks into the Sky Ocean and does not return.
- **First Appearance**: Arc 4 Ch 17 — Kaelen Mor. The Rain Listener who refused. Half-coral, half-human, still conscious. *"I ran… but the sea doesn't forget. It just waits."*
- **Narrative Use**: Shows the cost of refusing the calling. Makes Cael's choice more desperate — the alternatives are madness (Kaelen) or sacrifice (Tide-Key). There is no third option.

## PART 3: ECOLOGICAL PHENOMENA (5 Types)

---

### 1. Bitter Rain (ฝนขม)
- **Description**: Rain with high salinity. When it falls on soil, normal plants die within days. Only hardy vegetation survives.
- **Ecological Impact**: Agricultural land shrinks year by year. Cities dependent on "rain gardens" — hydroponic farms growing tolerant crops. Food scarcity is a constant background pressure.
- **First Appearance**: Arc 1 Ch 2 (Flashback) — The rain Nio touches.
- **Plot Use**: Economic pressure driving cities toward dependence on Sky-Dam energy. Background worldbuilding — the rain is slowly starving everyone, not just mutating the unlucky.

---

### 2. The Shroud (ม่านหมอก)
- **Description**: A dense fog layer between the earth's surface and the Sky Ocean. Thickness varies — at its densest, visibility is zero. Airships must use high speed to "punch through." Temperature inside the Shroud drops below freezing. Strange sounds echo in the fog — possibly the Leviathan, possibly the Drowned Choir, possibly just wind.
- **Ecological Impact**: The physical barrier separating the world below from the world above. Makes Sky Ocean travel dangerous and expensive.
- **First Appearance**: Arc 1 Ch 5 — The *Albatross* ascends through it.
- **Plot Use**: Transition between worlds. Danger during ascent/descent. Isolation — once inside the Shroud, you are truly cut off from both earth and sky.

---

### 3. Ash-Blooms (ดอกเถ้าบาน)
- **Description**: Phenomenon where seawater vapor on the Sky Ocean's surface crystallizes into flower-like formations. Beautiful — intricate pale structures that refract light into rainbow patterns. Release toxic gas on contact with air.
- **Ecological Impact**: Blooms kill birds that fly too low. Ships that sail through them suffer crew poisoning.
- **First Appearance**: Arc 2 Ch 7 — The Sky Ocean. Ilyra steers around an Ash-Bloom field.
- **Plot Use**: Visual beauty masking danger — a microcosm of the story's theme. Environmental obstacle.

---

### 4. Tide-Singing (เสียงกระแสน้ำ)
- **Description**: Phenomenon where water in pipes, rivers, and drainage systems begins to "sing" at the Leviathan's frequency. Grows louder as the Sky-Dam weakens. Heard even in cities far from the Sky Ocean — the Leviathan's call propagates through all connected water.
- **Ecological Impact**: Psychological — people who hear Tide-Singing report nightmares and feelings of being "called" somewhere.
- **First Appearance**: Arc 2 Ch 7 — Faintly. Arc 3 Ch 11 — Sera hears it in the archive pipes.
- **Plot Use**: Mystery seed. Signal that the Leviathan is waking. Countdown mechanism — the louder the Tide-Singing, the closer the Sky-Dam is to collapse.

### 5. The Descent Zone (เขตทะเลตก)
- **Description**: Areas where water from the Sky Ocean begins to "leak" downward as the Sky-Dam fails. Appears as dense cold fog. Temperature drops. Sky Ocean creatures — small fish, Pale-Moths, Glass Eels — fall through with the water.
- **Ecological Impact**: Death zone for anything beneath it. Soil becomes saturated with salt. Structures collapse under water weight. The first sign of the final catastrophe.
- **First Appearance**: Arc 5 Ch 25 — The Sky-Dam begins to crack. First Descent Zones form.
- **Last Appearance**: Arc 7 Ch 33 — The ocean is fully down. Descent Zones dissipate.
- **Plot Use**: Final countdown. The geography of disaster — which cities are under Descent Zones determines evacuation order. Elaris is first.

## PART 4: PREDATOR-CLASS SKY OCEAN CREATURES

### 11. Lure-Lanterns (ตะเกียงล่อเหยื่อ) — Layer 2+
- **Type**: Predatory cephalopod — Bioluminescent ambush hunter
- **Appearance**: Translucent bell (~60-90cm) with muscular tentacles. Emits warm golden light from center — indistinguishable from a lantern at 20m. Dims to near invisibility when not hunting.
- **Behavior**: Suspends in Drowned Quarter windows. Pulses light to mimic human habitation. When prey approaches, 8 tentacles strike — paralytic barbs. Prey remains conscious during digestion (3-6 hours).
- **Hunting Strategy**: Mimicry-based luring. Groups synchronize pulses to create illusion of inhabited structures. Some dim when prey looks directly at them — basic threat-awareness.
- **First Appearance**: Arc 2 Ch 7 — Cael sees lights in drowned city windows. Cassian: *"Those aren't lights. Don't look at them."*
- **Last Appearance**: Arc 5 Ch 21 — Explorer's Graveyard infested with Lure-Lanterns pulsing distress signals.
- **Danger Level**: HIGH
- **Narrative Use**: Ambush horror. "Candlelit window in a dead city." Thematic: even the light here is a lie.

### 12. Husk-Takers (ตัวเลียนแบบ) — Layer 2
- **Type**: Colony organism — Shapeshifting ambush predator
- **Appearance**: Salt-crusted mound (~1-1.5m) at rest. When hunting: crude human silhouette — no face, crystalline texture, slight shimmer. The silhouette waves.
- **Behavior**: Nests in doorways. Mimics human shape — "person waving for help." When prey approaches, colony collapses inward — salt-crystal tendrils engulf prey. External digestion.
- **Origin**: Fungal colony that fed on human corpses. Evolved to mimic its primary food source. Not intelligent — purely reactive.
- **First Appearance**: Arc 5 Ch 21 — Cassian fires at a waving silhouette. It dissolves into salt crystals.
- **Danger Level**: MEDIUM — Easily avoided if recognized. Deadly if approached.
- **Narrative Use**: Psychological horror. "Person in the window" corrupted.

### 13. Trench-Wyrms (มังกรร่องลึก) — Layer 3
- **Type**: Serpentine pack predator — High tactical intelligence
- **Appearance**: 2-4m serpentine body. Translucent skin with blue bioluminescent veins. No eyes — navigates by pressure-sensing (±100m). Fins flash to coordinate attacks.
- **Behavior**: Hunt in packs of 4-12. Coordinated flanking maneuvers. Test prey before committing — send one to probe, observe, then assault. They learn from encounters.
- **Hunting Strategy**: Pack tactics — encircle → probe → assault weakest target. Retreat and observe if assault fails.
- **First Appearance**: Arc 5 Ch 22 — Pack circles *Albatross* during emergency descent. Cassian: *"They're measuring us."*
- **Danger Level**: VERY HIGH
- **Narrative Use**: Intelligent predator — not a monster but a thinking enemy.

### 14. Void-Anglers (ตะเกียงเหว) — Layer 4
- **Type**: Gigantic ambush predator — Deep-layer apex
- **Appearance**: 20-40m bell. Light organ illuminates small-settlement-sized area. Can mimic fleet lights, Ash-Bloom, or false dawn. Tentacles 60-80m — near invisible in dark.
- **Behavior**: Creates false environments — fake ship lights, fake sky, fake safety. Prey does not realize it's descending until tentacles close.
- **First Appearance**: Arc 5 Ch 25 — Nio perceives through Leviathan: *"A false sky in the deep."*
- **Danger Level**: APEX — No survival strategy. Avoid.
- **Narrative Use**: Cosmic horror. The "light at the end of the tunnel" is a stomach.

## PART 5: HYBRID BEINGS — POST-HUMAN

### 15. The Ash-Turned (ผู้ถูกเถ้ากลืน) — Ground / Mistwood
- **Type**: Post-human — Brine-touched with zero cognitive function
- **Appearance**: Humanoid fusion — gill-slits, webbing, patchwork scales/exposed muscle. Jerky movement. Constant low moan at Leviathan frequency.
- **Behavior**: Mindless. Wanders. Bites transmit brine-touch. Groups synchronize moaning into localized Drowned Choir.
- **Origin**: Brine-touched isolated 2-5 years. Leviathan's call erases cognition. Body keeps mutating.
- **First Appearance**: Arc 3 Ch 14 — One wears Elaris dockworker uniform. Odran recognizes it. Does not speak.
- **Danger Level**: MEDIUM — Groups are extremely dangerous.
- **Narrative Use**: "What could happen to Nio." Odran's guilt — some wear uniforms of children he sent to Salt Ward.

### 16. Coral Husks (เปลือกปะการังเดินได้) — Pearl Colony
- **Type**: Post-human — Fully calcified but mobile
- **Appearance**: Humanoid coral-encased. Face visible under translucent coral layer — mouth frozen in silent scream. Move slowly with cracking sounds.
- **Behavior**: Drawn to warmth. Stand motionless outside windows. Contact transfers Coral-Bone spores.
- **Origin**: ~1 in 20 calcified victims become Husks. Warden considers them "imperfect specimens."
- **First Appearance**: Arc 4 Ch 17 — Husk stands in team's path. Maera: *"Is it… alive."*
- **Danger Level**: LOW directly — EXTREME as infection vector.
- **Narrative Use**: Horror of failed transformation. Dark counterpart to Warden's "beautiful garden."

### 17. Drowned Walkers (ผู้เดินใต้น้ำ) — Layer 2 / Corpse Fields
- **Type**: Post-human — Glass Eel colony hosts in preserved corpses
- **Appearance**: Salt-preserved corpses animated by Glass Eels inside. Jerky locomotion — limbs move wrong directions. Eels visible through translucent skin — blue threads pulsing.
- **Behavior**: Drawn to living bioelectric fields. Eels exit corpse and enter living host on contact.
- **First Appearance**: Arc 5 — Ilyra recognizes a Walker's face: a fellow salvager, dead 5 years.
- **Danger Level**: MEDIUM
- **Narrative Use**: Ilyra's personal horror. Thematic: even the dead cannot rest.

## PART 7: ADDITIONAL PARASITES & CONDITIONS

### 7. The Half-State (สภาวะกึ่งกลาง)
- **Cause**: Artificially interrupted brine-touch transformation. Dead Parliament experiments.
- **Symptoms**: Body trapped between human and brine-touched. One non-functional gill. Scales on one side only. Salt-water blood causing constant pain. Cannot breathe air or water properly. Beg for death.
- **First Appearance**: Arc 5 Ch 22 — Nio perceives Half-State victims: *"They're not alive… but they can't die."*
- **Narrative Use**: Dead Parliament's cruelty — they "paused" the experiment.

### 8. Bone-Silt Borer (หนอนไขกระดูก) — Parasitic Worm
- **Type**: Parasitic worm — Bone marrow specialist
- **Life Cycle**: Larvae enter through contaminated water or wounds in Brine-Spore environments. Migrate to bone marrow, mature there, consume marrow and replace it with brittle calcium deposits. Survive host death — must be burned with body.
- **Symptoms**: Veins harden into visible white lines. Movement causes internal bleeding from shards. Terminal: heart pumps calcified slurry.
- **First Appearance**: Arc 3 Ch 12 — Salt Ward subjects. Doctor Prahm considers them "useful complications."
- **Narrative Use**: Body horror. The Salt Ward breeds parasites that breed more parasites.
