# 🎙️ Voice & Style Principles: The Sea That Hung Above The World

This document defines the stylistic rules and voice guidelines for the project. Both human writers and AI agents must follow these rules to maintain stylistic coherence and avoid generic prose.

---

## 👁️ Narrative Stance & Perspective

- **Point of View**: Shifting Third-Person Limited (Cael, Ilyra, Sera, Nio, Maera).
- **POV Structure**: Locked at exactly 5 POV characters:
  - **Cael Arvane** (Active Present - Suppressed ENFJ): Focuses on moisture, rainfall frequencies, temperature, and signs of mutation (pale water-stains on wrists, breathing).
  - **Ilyra Venn** (Active Present - ENFP): Focuses on wind direction, rigging wear, ship weight, flight speeds, and the horizon.
  - **Maera Sol** (Active Present - ISTP): Focuses on mechanical structures, leverage, torque, friction, engine pressure, and Tovin's physical actions/injuries.
  - **Sera Quill** (Active Present - INTJ): Focuses on documents, ink age, map coordinates, official seals, and Ministry propaganda.
  - **Nio Arvane** (Past Flashbacks & Present Active - INFP):
    - *Past Flashbacks*: Human-focused, childhood memories, the cold sensation of the forbidden rain, and the horror of the Salt Ward containment cells.
    - *Present Active*: Mutated, non-human, abstract sensory filters. Focuses on whale song frequencies, bone-calcium vibrations, the thoughts of the Leviathan, and the shifting tides.
- **Psychic Distance**: Close/Deep POV. The narration must be filtered strictly through the active character's knowledge state, biases, and sensory limitations.
- **Observing the Leader**: To maximize Cassian Veyr's "aura" of leadership, he is strictly a non-POV character. His decisive actions, absolute calm under pressure, and mental anchor role are observed through the eyes of Maera and Sera (in Team Cassian) and Cael and Ilyra (in Team Cael), making his competence feel mythic and earned rather than narrated in the first-person.

## ✍️ Prose Rhythm & Sentence Patterns

- **Sentence Variety**:
  - Short, fragmented sentences for moments of body horror, panic, or claustrophobia (e.g., *Metal in the throat. Breath caught. A sound like drowning.*).
  - Longer, flowing sentences when describing the vast, terrifying beauty of the Sky Ocean or characters reflecting on their past.
- **Show vs. Tell**:
  - Avoid abstract emotional labels like *"He was afraid,"* *"She felt sad,"* or *"They were shocked."*
  - Instead, show fear and sadness through perception, avoidance, and physical reaction (e.g., *His collar felt damp. He didn't look at the glove. The pale stain spread at the corner of his eye, stiffening as it dried.*).
  - Filter words to avoid: *saw, heard, felt, realized, noticed, knew*. Write the observation directly (e.g., write *A whale song shook the deck* instead of *She heard a whale song shake the deck*).

---

## 🎨 Sensory Grounding & Details

- **Primary Sensory Anchors**:
  - **Scent**: Sulfur, rotting kelp, wet wood, metallic copper, the ozone of deep water.
  - **Sound**: The dull vibration of whale songs through the hull, the drip of condensation, the wind whistling through rigging, the crackle of drying brine on skin.
  - **Touch**: The weight of damp clothes, the cold, smooth glass of Nio's mutated skin, the itch of old water-stains.
- **Rule of Thumb**: Select one or two highly specific sensory details for each new setting rather than listing everything. What the character notices must reveal who they are.

---

## 🗣️ Dialogue & Subtext

- **Dialogue Friction**: Characters should rarely state their exact feelings or motivations. Use dialogue to hide secrets, lie, or test boundaries.
- **Tags & Beats**:
  - Minimize dialogue tags (e.g. *said, asked*). Use action beats to ground the characters in their physical task (e.g., *Ilyra pulled the knot tight. "We need to move."* instead of *"We need to move," she said, pulling the knot.*).
  - Never use dramatic dialogue tags (*shouted, demanded, wept, explained*). Let the words and beats convey the emotion.

### Character Voice Differentiation
- **Cael**: Short, hesitant sentences, uses language only when necessary. Quiet, polite, under immense pressure.
- **Ilyra**: Rapid, colloquial speech, talks to cover her anxiety. Defiant and humorous.
- **Sera**: Precise, analytical vocabulary, speaks in terms of coordinates, records, and concrete facts. Sharp-tongued.
- **Maera**: Prickly, dry, and highly technical. Refuses to use emotional words (like *worry, care, love*). When concerned, she scolds. Uses mechanical terms (*torque, clearance, fuel, friction*).
- **Tovin**: Simple, warm, and self-deprecating. Uses ordinary language, focuses on human needs (*soup, sleep, heat*). Acts as a conversational contrast to the grand, poetic vocabulary of the mythic characters.
- **Cassian**: ENTJ Analyst. Decisive, strategic, and authoritative. Speaks in terms of large-scale logistics, tactical advantages, and macro-objectives. He avoids emotional pleading, speaking with calm, quiet authority. His actions define him. His humor is deadpan and unintentional, stemming from extreme literalism. He treats civilian banter, sarcasm, and Tovin's blockheaded attempts at romance/mechanics with serious tactical analysis, creating natural situational comedy.
- **Loran**: ESTJ Sentinel. Authoritative, direct, and highly organized. Speaks using precise aviation and military navigation terminology (*"vector," "bearing," "payload," "descent"*). He views duty as a concrete responsibility to his dependents rather than an abstract ideal; his speech pattern is blunt, demanding order, and rejecting Cassian's ENTJ strategic martyrdom as an illogical waste of leadership.
