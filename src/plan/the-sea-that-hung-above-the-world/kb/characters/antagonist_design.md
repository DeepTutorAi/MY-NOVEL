# ⚙️ Antagonist Design

This document details the opposing forces: the historical archives of the Dead Parliament and the present-day guardian, Marrow Saint.

---

## 🏛️ Past Antagonists: The Ascendant Engineers & The Dead Parliament

### 1. The Ascendant Engineers
- **Philosophy**: Utilitarian survival.
- **Background**: Ancient humans who lived during a resource war that left the earth dying. Desperate to save humanity, they built the **Sky-Dam Engine** to lift the oceans, using the pressure, salt, and aquatic life as a colossal power source. They saved humanity from extinction but left the planet a breathing corpse.

### 2. The Dead Parliament
- **Mechanism**: A consciousness archive of the Engineers' leaders, scientists, and generals, embedded directly into the Sky-Dam Engine.
- **Threat**: Though their bodies are dead, their minds command the engine. Their voice represents the automated law of the past, whispering: *"Human survival outranks planetary pain."* They view the ocean's return as a system failure that must be suppressed by force.

---

## 🛡️ Present Antagonists: The Ministry & Marrow Saint

### 1. The Ministry of Dry Earth
- **Role**: The military/clerical government of the remaining cities.
- **Method**: Direct information suppression. They teach that the Sky Ocean is a demon, rain is a curse, and anyone who can hear the water is "contaminated."
- **Motivation**: Power and resources. The cities run on the energy channeled from the Sky-Dam. If the ocean falls, the Ministry's monopoly on energy, maps, and safety collapse.

### 2. Marrow Saint
- **Age**: 70+
- **Appearance**: Tall, thin, and elegant, carrying himself with the quiet authority of an old ruler. Silvery white hair and pale, cold eyes.
- **Personality**: INTJ/INFJ. Calm, brilliant, polite, and dangerous. He speaks in a soft, gentle tone, never raising his voice.
- **Want**: Maintain the Sky-Dam and the system of lies at all costs.
- **Belief**: Humanity is too fragile to survive the truth. If the truth is revealed, chaos will kill everyone before they can adapt.
- **The Core Truth**: He is partially right. Humanity *will* struggle, and many *will* die during the transition. This makes his arguments reasonable and terrifying to Cael.
- **Route**:
  - *Early*: A distant authority behind the Ministry's decrees.
  - *Middle*: Confronts Cael and offers a logical temptation: *"Humanity will drown before it can repair the world. Fix the dam, and save your brother."*
  - *Late*: Strives to maintain control as the dam fractures. He fails not because of incompetence, but because he underestimates the capacity of ordinary humans to cooperate and change.
- **Final State**: Dies with the old machinery, abandoned in a control room that no longer commands obedience.
